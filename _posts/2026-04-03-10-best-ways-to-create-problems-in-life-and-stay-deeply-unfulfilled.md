A brutally honest guide to suffering — that you're probably already following.

You don't need bad luck to have a hard life.
You don't need enemies. You don't need circumstances to conspire against you.
You just need to follow the playbook most people follow — unconsciously, automatically, every single day.
Here it is. The complete guide.

1. **Practice being stressed all the time:**
   Make it your default setting.
   Wake up stressed. Commute stressed. Work stressed. Eat stressed. Sleep — if you can — stressed.
   Don't question it. Don't examine it. Just assume this is what life feels like when you're "busy" or "ambitious" or "responsible."
   Stress isn't a signal that something's wrong. It's proof you're working hard.
   Right?
2. **Complain about everything:**
   Especially the things you can't control.
   The weather. The traffic. The economy. Your boss. Your team. Your family. The government. The algorithm.
   Complain often. Complain loudly. Complain to anyone who will listen — and keep going when they stop listening.
   Never ask: What can I actually do about this?
   That's not the point. The point is to feel right. To feel wronged.
3. **Overthink every decision at least 10 times:**
   Then doubt the conclusion anyway.
   Should I take this job? Should I leave this job? Should I say yes? Should I say no? What will they think? What if I'm wrong? What if I regret it?
   Run through every possible outcome. Then run through them again. Then imagine three more outcomes that will never happen — and worry about those too.
   Make sure no decision ever feels clean. Make sure there's always a residue of doubt sitting underneath every choice.
4. **When someone says something you don't like — lash out:**
   Lose your cool. Say something you'll regret. Let the reaction run the show.
   Don't pause. Don't breathe. Don't ask yourself: Is this the person I want to be right now?
   Just react. Let the anger or hurt or frustration express itself immediately, unfiltered.
   Then spend the next 48 hours replaying the conversation and wishing you'd handled it differently.
5. **Be upset when life doesn't go your way:**
   Refuse to see what it's already offering.
   When the plan falls apart, don't adapt. Resist. Resent.
   When the door closes, don't look for another one. Stand in front of the closed door and demand it reopen.
   Never ask: What is this making possible that wasn't possible before?
   That's too mature. Too surrendered. Too calm.
   Stay upset. It's more familiar.
6. **Let fear and self-doubt run the show**
   Call it "being realistic."
   Don't go for the thing you actually want — you'll probably fail anyway. Don't speak up in the meeting — someone smarter will say it better. Don't start the project — who are you to think you could pull it off?
   Dress up fear as caution. Dress up self-doubt as humility.
   Stay small. Stay safe. Stay exactly where you are.
7. **Keep replaying the mistakes you made:**
   Punish yourself daily.
   That thing you said five years ago? Still cringe. That opportunity you missed? Still stings. That relationship you fumbled? Still hurts.
   Don't let yourself off the hook. Don't consider that you were doing the best you could with what you knew at the time.
   Hold yourself hostage to every past version of yourself. Refuse to let them evolve.
8. **Compare your chapter 2 to someone else's chapter 20:**
   Feel terrible about it.
   Scroll through LinkedIn. Look at the promotions. The exits. The accolades.
   Don't consider their decade-long head start. Don't consider what they sacrificed or inherited or lucked into.
   Just compare. Just feel behind.
   Make your own progress invisible by constantly measuring it against someone else's highlight reel.
9. **Wait for the perfect moment:**
   It will never come. Keep waiting.
   Don't start until you're ready. Don't launch until it's perfect. Don't move until every variable is accounted for.
   Wait for clarity. Wait for confidence. Wait for certainty.
   And while you wait — watch others stumble forward, learn, iterate, and succeed while you're still preparing.
10. **Curse life for being unfair:**
    Ignore everything that's working.
    Focus on the gap, not the gain. Focus on the lack, not the presence.
    Wake up in a safe home, in a body that works, with people who care — and spend your first conscious breath thinking about what's missing.
    Gratitude is naïve. Optimism is for people who haven't seen the real world.
    Stay bitter. It suits you.

The uncomfortable truth
Read that list again.
How many of those are you doing — right now — without realizing it?
Not because you're broken. Not because you're weak. Not because you lack intelligence.
But because you've never been shown another way.
Most people use their intelligence against themselves. They overthink instead of solve. They worry instead of act. They replay instead of move forward.


We call this Destructive Intelligence — the same powerful mind that could be building, creating, and solving... turned inward. Looping. Attacking. Exhausting.
It's not a character flaw. It's a pattern. And patterns can be broken.

There's another way to operate
What if your intelligence actually worked for you?
What if decisions came with clarity instead of noise?
What if stress wasn't your default — but something you could actually turn off?
What if the mental chatter, the self-doubt, the overthinking... just stopped?
This is what we call Constructive Intelligence. The same mind — but directed. Intentional. Rested.

It's not about working harder. It's about removing the friction you didn't know was there.

Or... you could try the opposite
Everything on that list has an inversion.
Stress → Stressless way of working.
Complaining → Clarity.
Overthinking → Decisive action.
Reacting → Responding.
Resisting life → Flowing with it.


The shift isn't conceptual. It's not a mindset hack. It's not "positive thinking."
It's a felt, measurable change in how your mind operates — and you can experience it in a single session.
